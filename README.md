# AI Well-being Research

## 📌 Research Title

**Relationship Between AI Usage, Trust in AI, Online Vigilance and Digital Well-Being: A Correlational Study.**

---

##  Overview

This research project investigates the relationship between individuals' motivations for using AI, their level of trust in AI systems, their online vigilance, and overall digital well-being.

The study focuses on **Indian young adults (18–25 years old)** and aims to contribute to the growing literature on psychological and behavioral impacts of AI usage.

---

##  Objectives

* To examine correlations between:

  * AI usage motives
  * Trust in AI
  * Online vigilance
  * Digital well-being
* To assess the reliability of psychological scales used
* To evaluate statistical assumptions such as normality

---

##  Sample

* **Sampling method:** Non-probability convenience sampling
* **Total responses collected:** 153
* **Valid responses used:** 143
* **Population:** Indian young adults (18–25 years)

---

##  Methodology

* Study Design: **Correlational research design**
* Data Collection Tool: **Google Forms**
* Data Analysis Software: **Python**

---

##  Statistical Analysis

The following analyses were performed:

* Descriptive Statistics
* Normality Testing (Shapiro–Wilk Test)
* Pearson's Product-Moment Correlation
* Significance Testing (p-values)
* Reliability Analysis (Cronbach’s Alpha)

2. Install required Python libraries (pandas, numpy, scipy, etc.)
3. Run:

   * `data_cleaning.py` → for preprocessing
   * `analysis_with_reverse_score.py` → with reverse scoring
   * `analysis_without_reverse_score.py` → without reverse scoring
